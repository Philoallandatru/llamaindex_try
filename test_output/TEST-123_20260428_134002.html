While Samsung often improves reliability with updates, they can inadvertently introduce performance bottlenecks if the new firmware optimizes for different workloads (e.g., prioritizing latency over throughput or changing queue depth limits). NVM Express Specification Constraints: According to the NVM Express Base Specification 2.1, the controller must adhere to specific command handling rules (referenced in Figure 183 regarding firmware status and Figure 595 regarding self-tests). If v3.3 introduced stricter validation or more frequent internal self-test operations during active I/O (as hinted at by Section 8.1.7/8.1.11 of the spec), it could introduce the observed latency increase (50µs → 80µs). If v3.3 alters how the controller utilizes available lanes or handles command bursting, it may result in the sequential read drop from 3500 MB/s to 2800 MB/s (approx. a 20% reduction), indicating inefficient data throughput management rather than a physical link failure. Lack of Regression Testing: The jump in latency and IOPS suggests that v3.3 may have relaxed certain error thresholds or changed the default queue depth handling, leading to suboptimal performance under the specific Ubuntu 22.04 driver configuration.
